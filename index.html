Hi! I'm Jackson Couse, an artist from Ottawa, Canada.

I make socially-engaged art: I interact with people to tell stories and make things. I work close to home, in my neighborhood, and on the street. Sometimes I work with strangers and sometimes with people I know. Making pictures, books, and videos is a way good to know people and the world. I like thinking and talking about things like identity, community, family, place, and performance.

When I'm not making art projects, I run a small press devoted to publishing projects from the social landscape.

I also teach workshops - with lots of interaction and participation - in photography and film-making.

If you'd like more information about me, I have a longer, more formal, third-person autobiography and an artist statement. Otherwise, please read on below for my projects and activities.

Selected Projects

I work in projects. Usually I have a few on the go at once, each taking several months to several years to complete.

  • Sociological Walk

    The Sociological Walk was a performance, intervention, and investigation exploring community life in the Somerset West neighborhood in Ottawa, Canada, where I grew up.

  • Queen's Diner

    For a couple of years I lived in Queens, New York, and around the corner from my house was Hugue and Sara's diner: Magazine Wells. Hugue was from Quebec, Sara from Queens. The diner was the best of both worlds, and my second home (and a lot of other people's, too).

  • Home Ice

    I was living in New York and feeling pretty homesick, so I started going to hockey games. Home Ice was made at Aviator Arena in Brooklyn, New York, during home games of the New York Aviators, a semi-pro hockey team.

  • Playing House

    My first long-term project. Fait Maison (French for "home made") was an art collective and performance series that took place in a house in Gatineau, Quebec. I photographed their performances for three years.
